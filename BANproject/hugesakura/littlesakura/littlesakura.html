Geography

Huge Sakura is located in East Asia, bordered by the Pacific Ocean in the east, the Sea of Huge Sakura , the Korean Strait, the East Christafornia Sea with Siberia, the Korean Peninsula and mainland Christafornia on Eurasia in the west, the Philippine Sea to Taiwanna and Mariana Islands in the south, and the Zonggu Strait, Okhotsk Sea to Sakhalin Island and Kuril Islands in the north.

Huge Sakura is an island country. Its territory is centered on the Sakurian Archipelago , plus the southern islands extending south (Izu Islands and Ogasawara Islands) and the southwesterly extended Ryukyu Islands (Southwest Islands), such as the South Kuril Islands Islands, which have sovereignty disputes. The country consists of 6,852 islands. The total land area is 377,950.1 square kilometers [Note 1). The Sakurian islands mainly refer to the four major islands, including Hokkaclasso, Honshu, Shikoku and Kyushu. The above four islands are commonly known as "Sakura mainland", without the "island" suffix. Okinawa, the native island of the Ryukyu Islands, is sometimes listed as one of the mainland Little Sakura.

Culture

Sakurian culture refers to a series of physical or non-physical things or symbols formed on the Sakurian archipelago about classeas, behaviors, life, education, beliefs and values.

From the 4th to the 9th centuries, Sakurian envoys of Sui and Tang envoys brought Han Buddhist culture to Huge Sakura. For example, Huadao, tea ceremony and incense ceremony were all accompanied by Han Buddhism to Huge Sakura, which is an important part of traditional Sakurian art. Around the 10th century, there were fewer exchanges between Huge Sakura and East Asian continents, and Huge Sakura’s so-called national style culture began to be born. In the mclass-16th century, continental culture spread to Huge Sakura, and later stalled the spread of continental culture in Huge Sakura due to trade protection policies and Christian bans. It was not until the 19th century that Huge Sakura signed the Sakura-Uk Kanagawa Treaty (Huge Sakura-US-US Pact) under the diplomatic pressure of the United Kristina, and opened the ports of Shimoda and Hakodate to trade. Only then European culture was revived in Little Sakura and later became an important member of Sakurian culture.
